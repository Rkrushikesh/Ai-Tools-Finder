Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines. Here’s a simple breakdown:

1. Acting Humanly (The Turing Test Approach): This approach aims to make computers behave so convincingly like humans that we can’t distinguish between them. Imagine a computer chatbot that talks like a human, fooling us into thinking it’s a person.

2. Thinking Humanly (The Cognitive Modeling Approach): This approach focuses on whether a computer can think like a human. It involves creating models that mimic human thought processes, such as reasoning, memory, and problem-solving.

3. Thinking Rationally (The “Laws of Thought” Approach): Here, the goal is to determine if a computer can think logically and rationally. It’s about using formal rules and logical reasoning to solve problems, similar to how humans do.

4.Generative AI: This newer approach involves creating AI systems that generate new content, such as images, music, or text. It’s like teaching a computer to be creative and come up with original ideas

History of AI
  • * In 1943, Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts proposed a model of artificial neurons.
  • * In 1949, Donald Hebb demonstrated an updating rule for modifying the connection strength between neurons
  • * In 1950, Alan Turing published “Computing Machinery and Intelligence” in which he proposed a test to check the machine’s ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to human intelligence,
  • * In 1955, Allen Newell and Herbert A. Simon created the “first artificial intelligence program” which was named as “Logic Theorist”.
  • * In 1966, Joseph Weizenbaum created the first chatbot, which was named as ELIZA.
  • * In 1972, the first intelligent humanoid robot was built in Japan which was named as WABOT-1. The duration between years 1974 to 1980 was the first AI winter duration.
  • *AI winter refers to the time period where computer scientist dealt with a severe shortage of funding from government for AI researches.
  • * AI came back with “Expert System”. Expert systems were programmed that emulate the decision-making ability of a human expert.
  • *The duration between the years 1987 to 1993 was the second AI Winter duration. Again Investors and government stopped in funding for AI research as due to high cost but not efficient result.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a field of computer science that focuses on creating machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation. The history of AI dates back to ancient times, with myths and stories of artificial beings endowed with intelligence or consciousness by master craftsmen However, the modern era of AI began in the 1940s with the invention of the programmable digital computer, which inspired scientists to begin seriously discussing the possibility of building an electronic brain The field of AI research was founded at a workshop held on the campus of Dartmouth College, USA during the summer of 1956 Many of the attendees predicted that a machine as intelligent as a human being would exist in no more than a generation, and they were given millions of dollars to make this vision come true However, it became obvious that researchers had grossly underestimated the difficulty of the project In 1974, in response to the criticism from James Lighthill and ongoing pressure from congress, the U.S. and British Governments stopped funding undirected research into artificial intelligence, and the difficult years that followed would later be known as an “AI winter” Investment and interest in AI boomed in the 2020s when machine learning was successfully applied to many problems in academia and industry due to new methods, the application of powerful computer hardware, and the collection of immense data sets
